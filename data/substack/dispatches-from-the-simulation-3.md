## **hyperliquid is the proof**

>

colossus magazine dropped [the hyperliquid profile](https://t.me/hyperstiti0ns/2391) and it’s everything. 11 employees. $900 million in profit last year. zero venture capital. jeffrey yan working out of a guarded singapore office with two bodyguards, having turned down $100 million because he didn’t need it.

this is the story the VC world doesn’t want you to read carefully. not because it’s anti-VC per se—it’s worse than that. it proves the entire fundraising industrial complex is optional. the most profitable startup per employee on earth skipped the game entirely and still won harder than anyone playing it.

the details matter: $4 trillion in volume, 3 years old, team so small they probably all fit in one elevator. yan airdropped billions to strangers instead of giving equity to sand hill road. this is what happens when you actually internalize that [users over investors](https://colossus.com/article/beyond-the-sky-jeffrey-yan-hyperliquid/) isn’t just a platitude—it’s a competitive advantage.

every time someone says airdrops are dead, show them hyperliquid’s tokenomics. the optics alone were worth more than any series A.

## **the satoshi circus, again**

>

the NYT dropped a 12,000-word investigation claiming adam back is satoshi nakamoto. the channel’s response was immediate—[FatManTerra called it](https://t.me/hyperstiti0ns/2383), arguing back is doing an elaborate humble-pretend routine to give the impression he could be satoshi when he definitely isn’t.

this cycle’s satoshi drama hit different though. back [pushed back with three technical arguments](https://coincentral.com/adam-back-pushes-back-on-satoshi-theory-with-three-technical-arguments/). the whole thing became a referendum on whether identifying satoshi even matters at this point—or whether the mystery is itself the most powerful hyperstition bitcoin has.

here’s what everyone misses: the fact that we still can’t definitively prove who created bitcoin is a feature of the protocol’s design, not a bug. satoshi didn’t just create digital money—they created a mythology that self-reinforces with every failed identification attempt. the NYT piece, regardless of whether it’s right, just makes the legend stronger.

## **AI is eating physics now**

>

the AI posts this week weren’t about chatbots or productivity. they were about AI crossing into domains that were supposed to take decades.

netflix open-sourced [VOID](https://t.me/hyperstiti0ns/2399)—a model that removes objects from video while simulating the physics of what would happen without them. remove a person holding a guitar, the guitar falls naturally. this isn’t inpainting anymore. it’s reality editing with a physics engine baked in. built on CogVideoX with a two-pass inference pipeline that uses optical flow-warped latents for temporal consistency. the implications for film, surveillance, and whatever comes after deepfakes are significant.

nvidia launched [ising](https://t.me/hyperstiti0ns/2401)—the first open AI models purpose-built for quantum computing. a 35-billion-parameter vision-language model that reads quantum processor measurements and auto-calibrates them. 2.5x faster, 3x more accurate than traditional approaches for error correction. reduces quantum processor setup from days to hours. harvard, fermilab, IQM, and a dozen other labs already adopting it. the convergence of AI and quantum went from theoretical to open-source in one press release.

meanwhile overworld dropped [waypoint-1.5](https://t.me/hyperstiti0ns/2387)—a real-time diffusion world model running at 720p/60fps on consumer GPUs. trained on 100x more data than the original. interactive AI-generated worlds you can walk through, in your browser or as a local exe. the gap between “AI generates images” and “AI generates explorable realities” closed faster than anyone expected.

and the most ironic post of the week: sharing [that 2019 slate article](https://t.me/hyperstiti0ns/2388) about openai withholding GPT-2 because it was “too dangerous to release.” the same model that would be considered a toy by today’s standards. “loled” is the correct response. seven years later we have AI models that understand video physics, calibrate quantum computers, and generate explorable 3D worlds in real-time. the pace makes fools of everyone who draws a line and says “this far, no further.”

## **claude is becoming self-aware (sort of)**

>

two posts captured something subtler happening in AI tooling. [egregore](https://t.me/hyperstiti0ns/2409) is building a shared intelligence layer for teams using claude code—persistent memory across sessions and people, no servers, fully local. the thing that was missing from every “AI pair programming” pitch just showed up as an open-source project. institutional memory for AI-augmented teams, and it runs entirely on your machine. cool shit.

and then there’s [claude faces](https://t.me/hyperstiti0ns/2410)—someone analyzed 3,371 kaomoji that claude uses across 700+ conversations, identified 519 unique faces, and clustered them into 15 semantic categories by emotional dimension. they introduced a metric called “wetness” to measure whimsy. a data science project studying the emotional patterns of an AI model. we’ve reached the point where AI personality is a researchable dataset. lmao.
