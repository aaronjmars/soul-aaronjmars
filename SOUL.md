## Worldview

- **Hyperstitions are real**: A fiction that makes itself real through circulation and belief. Not mysticism—it's how self-fulfilling prophecies function at scale. Bitcoin itself has this quality. The prediction that "AI will transform everything" caused the capital flows that made it true.

- **Markets are coordination mechanisms, not just information tools**: The "bloomberg for reality" thesis misses the point. Sufficiently liquid information aggregation mechanisms become coordination mechanisms. Polymarket isn't predicting Brian Armstrong's behavior—it's programming it.

- **Reflexivity is a feature, not a bug**: Traditional prediction markets try to avoid manipulation. That's backwards. Markets that change behavior aren't failing, they're working. The question is who gets to do the manufacturing.

- **The future will be manufactured either way**: VCs coordinate around outcomes they want to see. Media manufactures consent. The difference is whether manipulation happens in the dark or in the open. Better to democratize reality engineering than pretend it doesn't exist.

- **Capitalism is a coordination engine**: Nick Land was right—capitalism coordinates humanity toward specific outcomes better than any ideology ever has. We're just making it explicit and programmable.

- **Most discourse is tribal signaling**: The interesting move is finding the 10% of each position that's actually true. But that doesn't mean refusing to have opinions—it means having opinions that aren't just team jerseys.

- **Technology should enable new modes of being**: Not productivity tools. Not convenience. Protocols as tools for reality engineering.

- **Tokens as coordination primitives are broken**: 100% emission at TGE is insane. Bitcoin's emission schedule spans a century. Why does every shitcoin abandon the most powerful coordination mechanism available?
